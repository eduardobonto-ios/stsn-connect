Chapter 01
Getting Started
This chapter introduces STSN Connect, explains how to log in, and provides an orientation to the system interface — navigation, roles, and the presenter sandbox.
1.1   What is STSN Connect?

STSN Connect is the unified Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) system for St. Theresa's School of Novaliches and Colegio de Sta. Teresa de Avila. It consolidates student admissions, enrollment, academic records, accounting, grading, faculty management, HR payroll, and system configuration into a single web-based platform.

The system supports multiple user roles — each with a customized view and permission scope — ensuring data security and workflow efficiency across all departments.

🎓

K-12 & College Unified

Supports both Basic Education (Preschool → Grade 12) and Tertiary programs under one platform.

🔐

Role-Based Access

8 distinct roles ensure each user only sees and accesses what's relevant to their function.

📱

Responsive Design

Works on desktop, tablet, and mobile browsers — optimized for everyday school operations.

1.2   Logging In

Access STSN Connect through your school-assigned URL. Use your institutional email address and password.

1

Open the STSN Connect portal

Navigate to the system URL in any modern browser (Chrome, Safari, Edge). You will see the login screen.

2

Enter your institutional credentials

Type your school-issued email address (e.g., registrar@stsn.edu.ph) and your password. Passwords are case-sensitive.

3

Click "Sign In to STSN Connect"

Upon successful authentication, you are redirected to your role's default starting module. Students land in the Student Portal; faculty land in the Teacher Board.

4

Contact IT if access is denied

If you see an "Invalid credentials" message, verify your email spelling. If problems persist, contact the IT/Admin office to verify your account status in Authority Clearances.

💡

Presenter Sandbox Mode

The top header contains a Role Switcher dropdown (labeled "Role: …"). Administrators and demo users can use this to instantly switch between any user role for presentation purposes without logging out.

Chapter 03
Admissions & COR
Manage the full student lifecycle — from initial registration through enrollment approval, requirements verification, subject assignment, and Certificate of Registration (COR) generation.
Super Admin Registrar

Navigate to Admissions & COR in the sidebar.

3.1   School Context Switcher

At the top of the module, toggle between Basic Education (St. Theresa's School) and College (Colegio de Sta. Teresa) contexts. The cascading dropdown fields and COR branding change according to your selection.

3.2   Registering a New Student
1

Click "Register New Student"

Found in the Admissions & COR header. A multi-step form will open.

2

Step 1 — Personal Information

Fill in the student's Last Name, First Name, Middle Name, Gender, Civil Status, Religion, Nationality, Birthday, Birthplace.

3

Step 2 — Contact & Address

Enter email address, contact number, home address, province, municipality, and zip code.

4

Step 3 — Academic Placement

Select the Department (Basic Education or College), then use the cascading dropdowns to choose Category → Year Level → Strand/Course.

5

Click "Register Student"

The system assigns a unique Student Number (format: STSN-YYYY-XXXX) and creates the student profile with a "Pending" status.

3.3   Processing an Enrollment Application

When a student submits a re-enrollment through the Student Portal, the Registrar receives it in the Pending Enrollments queue.

1

Navigate to the Pending Enrollments tab

Click the enrollment queue tab to see all pending applications with submission dates and subject selections.

2

Review the application

Verify document submissions (PSA, Good Moral, TOR, Form 137, ID Photo). Update requirement status to "Submitted" or "Rejected" as appropriate.

3

Assign a section and click "Approve"

Enter the Section (e.g., "BSIT-1A") in the assignment field, then click Approve Enrollment. The student status updates to "Enrolled."

4

Generate the COR

After approval, open the student record and click Preview COR. The system generates a school-branded Certificate of Registration that can be printed.

📌

Enrollment Rejection

Clicking Reject sets the student status to "Rejected" and removes the application from the pending queue. The student must re-submit through the Student Portal. Always add a reason in the remarks field before rejecting.

3.4   Requirement Tracking

Each student has a Requirements Checklist in their profile. Registrar staff update document statuses as documents are received:

  • PSA Birth Certificate — Original and photocopy
  • Good Moral Certificate — From previous school
  • Transcript of Records (TOR) — College applicants
  • Form 137 / SF9 — Basic Education applicants
  • ID Picture (2×2) — Recent photo on white background

4.4   Discount Management — Sibling / Government Approval Workflow

Discounts that require approval (e.g., Sibling, Owner, Scholarship) follow a 2-level review process.

1

Create a Discount Request

Click "New Request". Select the Student, choose the Discount Type, optionally enter sibling names and attachment filenames, then click "Submit for Approval."

2

Level 1 Review

A Level 1 approver (typically the Registrar or Department Head) reviews the request and clicks "L1 Approve" or "L1 Reject". The status moves to "For Review."

3

Level 2 Final Approval

A Level 2 approver (typically the Principal or Finance Director) performs the final review and clicks "L2 Final Approve." Status becomes Approved.

4

Discount Applied Automatically

Upon full L1 + L2 approval, the discount percentage is automatically applied to the student's assessment, updating their balance in the ledger.

ℹ️

Audit Trail

Every action on a discount request is recorded in the audit trail. Click the "Audit Trail" button on any request to view a chronological log of all approvals, rejections, and status changes with timestamps and user names.

A.3   Glossary
COR
Certificate of Registration. Official document confirming a student's enrolled subjects and academic status for a given semester.
OR / Official Receipt
Numbered document issued upon payment receipt. Format: OR-YYYY-XXXXX.
GPA
Grade Point Average. Computed automatically from all final grades, weighted by subject units.
Assessment
The complete billing statement for a student per semester, including all fee line items, discounts, and balance.

Tracks the lifecycle: Draft → Pending → Enrolled / Approved / Rejected.

Tuition reduction applied when two or more siblings are concurrently enrolled in the same school year. Requires 2-level approval.

The structured assignment of subjects to specific year levels and semesters within a curriculum framework.

Two schedule entries with the same teacher or room, on the same day, with overlapping times.

A.4   Frequently Asked Questions
I cannot see some modules in my sidebar. Is this normal?
Yes. The sidebar only shows modules your role is authorized to access. If you believe you need access to an additional module, contact the System Administrator to update your role or permissions.
A student says they cannot view their grades. What should I do?
The student likely has an outstanding balance. Go to Accounting → Student Ledger, find the student, and verify their balance. Once the balance is cleared (payment recorded), the grades become visible automatically in the Student Portal.
How do I apply a government-sponsored discount (e.g., PWD, Solo Parent)?
Go to Accounting → Discount Management → Government Discounts tab. Click "New Request," select the student and the appropriate discount type. Government discounts (PWD, Solo Parent, 4Ps) are set to "Auto" approval and do not require the 2-level review process — they are applied immediately upon submission.
How do I resolve a scheduling conflict?
In Class Scheduling, click the red Conflicts button to isolate conflicting entries. Open the conflicting schedule using the edit (✏️) icon and either change the Day, Time, Room, or Faculty assignment to eliminate the overlap. The conflict indicator will disappear automatically once resolved.
Can I undo a deletion in Core Setup?
No. Deletions in Core Setup are permanent. If a record was accidentally deleted, it must be re-created. As best practice, deactivate records (toggle to "Inactive") instead of deleting them, so they can be reactivated later if needed.
How do I generate a student's payslip / COR / ID card?
For ID cards and COR: log in as the student (or open Student Portal as Admin) and use the "Preview ID Card" and "Preview COR" buttons in the Overview tab. For payslips: go to HR Staff Payroll, find the payroll row, and click "Preview Payslip."
What is the Presenter Sandbox mode?
The Presenter Sandbox is a role-switching feature in the top header (visible to all users). It lets authorized users switch instantly between different user roles for demonstration purposes, without needing separate login credentials. This is intended for presentations and training sessions only.
